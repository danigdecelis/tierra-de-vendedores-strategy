The Problems

Now that we know what is there, here is what those findings reveal about what is costing you leads right now.

Problem 1 of 3

Every Visitor Disappears Without a Trace

Your site has no tracking and no capture mechanism. Every person who finds you through a LinkedIn post, a Google search, or a word-of-mouth referral and does not immediately book a call is gone permanently. You have no way to know they were there, no way to follow up, and no way to bring them back.

Here is what the buyer journey looks like today:

1
Prospecto reads a LinkedIn post
They recognize themselves in the content. The pipeline culture post, the iceberg framework, the tero bird case study. They think: this person understands my exact situation.
2
Visits tierradevendedores.com.ar
They review the testimonials, read about the methodology, and find the qualifying language compelling. They see themselves as the target client.
3
Not quite ready to commit to a full diagnosis call
Maybe they are mid-budget cycle. Maybe the timing is off by 30 days. They tell themselves they will come back later.
4
Closes the tab. Never comes back.
You do not know they were there. There is no pixel to retarget them. There is no email to follow up. When they are finally ready, they find someone else.
Every day without a capture system is a day your best-fit prospects exit permanently, including the ones who were one conversation away from becoming a client.
Problem 2 of 3

Your Only CTA Asks Too Much, Too Soon

The only path from your site and LinkedIn to a paid engagement is "Solicitar diagnostico." That is a significant ask from a prospect who just discovered you. Most consultants lose the majority of their interested audience at exactly this step, not because the offer is wrong, but because the timing is wrong and there is no bridge between curiosity and commitment.

The "Ready Right Now" Window Is Narrow

Most qualified B2B buyers who discover a consultant are not ready to commit to a diagnostic call on the same day. They need two or three more touchpoints first. Without a low-friction middle step, those prospects have nowhere to go.

A Manual Google Form Is Not a System

The pre-qualification form shared in LinkedIn posts requires manual follow-up for every submission. No automated confirmation, no nurture sequence, no scalable process. Every lead is a one-off manual effort.

Your Content Is Doing All the Work Alone

Your posts build trust daily. But trust without a system does not consistently close deals. The missing piece is a structured nurture process that keeps you in front of warm prospects over 30 to 90 days between "discovered you" and "ready to engage."

Every prospect who is interested today but not quite ready needs somewhere to land. Without a capture layer, they land with your competitors.
Problem 3 of 3

A 1,915-Follower Audience Built on Borrowed Ground

linkedin.com/in/coachgustavomassa

You have built something genuinely valuable on LinkedIn: a growing audience of business owners and commercial directors who engage with your content consistently. The problem is that audience belongs to LinkedIn, not to you. You have no portable, owned database of contacts that survives algorithm changes, platform policy shifts, or account restrictions.

What should be there instead

A dedicated landing page connected to your content, offering a high-value resource in exchange for an email address. Every person who downloads that resource enters a database that you own outright, that grows every week, and that you can reach regardless of what LinkedIn decides to do with its organic reach next month. That owned database is the asset that transforms your content activity into a durable business pipeline, rather than a social media presence that could disappear tomorrow.

Stop renting an audience. Start building one.
